## Domain 1: General Security Concepts

### Core Principles
* **Confidentiality, Integrity, and Availability (CIA):** The fundamental model for security policy development.
* **Non-Repudiation:** Verification that a specific action was taken by a specific user.
* **Zero Trust Architecture:** A security model centered on the belief that organizations should not automatically trust anything inside or outside its perimeters.

### Identity and Access Management (IAM)
* **Authentication vs. Authorization:** Verifying identity versus verifying permissions.
* **Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):** Utilizing multiple independent categories of credentials to verify a user's identity.
* **Principle of Least Privilege:** Restricting access rights for users to the bare minimum permissions they need to perform their work.

### Security Governance
* **Change Management:** Reducing the risk of unauthorized or unintended changes to the environment.
* **Asset Management:** Maintaining an inventory of authorized devices and software to ensure security compliance.
